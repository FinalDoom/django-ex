Professionally, I'd probably say my field is Computer Science, but as I'm travelling, my time goes mostly toward photography. I recently finished graduate school at RIT. My Bachelor's and Master's degrees are in Computer Science, and my undergraduate work included minors in Psychology and Japanese Language, plus classes and seminars in a variety of disciplines including photography. It's taken a long time, but my fields of study have given me considerable insight into my self and what I am driven by, among the other knowledge I've gained.

Probably the biggest factor in what I enjoy is that I like making things. This includes variety of activities, such as programming, woodworking and carpentry (specifically furniture making), gardening, cooking, and simply fixing things. Perhaps on a similar note, I also enjoy making a positive impact. I have an easy time getting along with people, and I like to use my background in psychology and human relations to help people with any kind of problem they may be having. I also value time spent improving myself in any way I can.

I have an affinity for languages, both computer and natural, and am reasonably proficient with English, Latin, and Japanese, and I'm working on German. I love to travel and experience things, and I am currently planning and engaging in a wandering trip around Europe and parts of Asia.

Before college, I spent 13 years training and playing classical piano. The creativity that allowed me has been missing for most of my time at college, and it has taken me some time to realize it. Hopefully, I will be able to get back into playing piano sooner than later. Furniture making and writing are two things I'm trying to give more focus, as they offer some creative outlet that is more readily available.

I'm an easy-going and open-minded person and am quite difficult to offend. By heredity, I am shy, so I tend not to talk a lot, except in certain circumstances and when it is needed, but if I am in a position to have a proper conversation with one person or am with people I'm comfortable with, I open up a lot. As someone who doesn't talk a lot, I listen to and observe people frequently and try to learn anything I can.
